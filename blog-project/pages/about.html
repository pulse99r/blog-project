About Kenti

Professional Musician, Entertainer and Music Instructor

Bio

June 13, 2022

Kenti is a professional musician, entertainer and music instructor with experience playing and teaching music (over 40 years playing and over 19 years teaching music on Guitar, Bass and Piano). Kenti’s knowledge, patience, professionalism, reliability and energy inspire students to successful achievement of mastery playing their instruments.

Kenti offers services in and around the Greater New York area, and is available for all occasions and events, including weddings, birthday parties, holiday events, corporate luncheons, cocktail hours, concert performances, and studio recording sessions, large and small and indoor or outdoor events.

Repertoire:

With a musical repertoire which includes jazz, rock, blues, reggae, folk, classical, pop, R&B, and bossa nova, Kenti‘s sets include standards from the American songbook, as well as some international, such as Italian, Jewish, Polish, Latin.

Kenti is currently accepting new students with skill levels that range from absolute beginners to advanced levels, and from ages three to adult. Lessons are offered seven days per week, all year-round. Kenti has two studio locations, midtown Manhattan and Mount Vernon in Westchester County. Additionally, Kenti offers lessons in the privacy of your own home where travel is convenient, and live, online video lessons will be available soon.

Education:

Kenti studied music at Febbraio School of Music in Westchester, New York

Anderson College (now Anderson University), in Anderson, Indiana

The School of Jazz in New York City

privately with noted music educator Ronny Lee in New York City.

Additionally had lessons with well-known guitarists and educators including, Howard Alden, Gene Bertoncini, John Hart, Sal Salvadore among others.

Influences:

I have been influenced by a number of different musical genres throughout my life. My first influence was church music. Then there was folk music. That was followed by the music of the Beatles, and the Brit Invasion. That was followed by music of the big rock bands like Led Zeppelin, Jefferson Airplane and Starship, Journey, Yes, The Who, Chicago, Kiss, and many others. That was followed in college by the jazz artists, Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington, Ella Fitzgerald, Billie Holiday, and others. I'm still a mostly jazz player today, however I also still love rock and roll and my performances and recordings include all the music to which I've been exposed.
